About Me

Learning, exploring, and growing along the way.

I am Yu Xu (徐煜), a master’s student in Basic Psychology at Shaanxi Normal University. My research interests lie in human decision-making, particularly the cognitive processes that shape choices across different contexts. In my current work, I use behavioral experiments, computational modeling, and neuroimaging methods to examine how people evaluate information, make decisions, and adjust their behavior based on previous experiences.

The questions that currently interest me include:

  • How does prior experience influence subsequent decision-making?
  • What latent cognitive processes underlie individual differences in choice behavior?
  • How can computational modeling be combined with neuroimaging methods to better understand decision processes?

Beyond academic work, I enjoy travel, photography, fitness, vocal music, choral singing, and games. These interests keep me curious, open-minded, and engaged with the world beyond research.
